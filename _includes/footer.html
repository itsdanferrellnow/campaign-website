We live on the land of the Tsalagi, who have called Shaconage home before the McDowells, the Pattons, the Chunns, the Reynolds, the Bairds, and the Merrimons. The city was named after Samuel Ashe, govenor of North Carolina, a major slaveowner who defended the right to slavery on the North Carolina Supreme Court. Each one of these families have names that haunt the streets of our city. We honor those who were here first to Togiyasdi, where the Tsalagi raced canoes where the French Broad and Swannanoa Rivers met, who's descendents were forcibly removed from their homes by the same Federal Government that seeks to separate families and destroy communities. We also want to acknowledge how the wealth of Asheville was built on chattel slavery, those families who leveraged brutality, starvation, coercion, and force for their own financial gain. We are committed to working with the Black community in Asheville, and the indigenous people in building collective liberation for all of us.
